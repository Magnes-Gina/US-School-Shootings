US School Shootings

Two decades ago, the United states witnessed the worst school shootings in its history prompting action on the relaxed gun laws in the country.

Fast Forward to 2020, the shootings have become deadlier such as Parkland and the government has turned a blind eye to the bloodshed.

Beginning with Columbine in 1999, more than 187,000 students attending at least 193 primary or secondary schools have experienced a shooting on campus during school hours, according to a year-long Washington Post analysis

There has been at least one school shooting every month in 2019. Less than three months into 2020, there were 18 school shootings.
With US Schools being shut down due to COVID-19, America has recorded a decline in school shootings for the first time in two decades.

The US makes up less than 5% of the world’s population, but holds 31% of global mass shooters.

Source: CNN

The number of civilian-owned firearms in the US is 88.8 guns per 100 people.

Source: CNN

The cost of US gun violence is almost $2.8 billion a year.

Source: Quartz

While NRA blames America’s mental health for school shootings, the National Public Radio identifies that mental health of students only plays as a risk factor and not as a main cause.

What makes a a small minority of students who have suicidal thoughts and mental issues turn to homicide?

When pain turns in to anger and a want for revenge…

Psychologists state that violent disputes among their peers lead them to homicidal thoughts and fantasies where they identify themselves with shooters who have seemingly solved their problems using violence. The Easy access to guns turns this revenge fantasy into a reality.
